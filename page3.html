LOGOs

Logos are visual symbols that represent a brand or business.
They communicate a brand’s values, mission, and personality.
Logos have the power to create recognition and leave a lasting impression.

BANNERS

a banner refers to a large, eye-catching display made of various materials such as fabric, vinyl, or paper.
It typically contains text, images, and graphics and is used for advertising,
promoting events, conveying messages, or enhancing brand visibility.

Cards

come in various forms and serve multiple purposes. They can be:
  • Greeting Cards: Used for occasions like birthdays, holidays, or anniversaries.
  • Business Cards: Essential for networking, providing contact information in a professional format.
  • Invitation Cards: For events such as weddings, parties, or corporate gatherings.

Brochures

Brochures are informative paper documents, often used for advertising. They can be:
  • Bi-fold or Tri-fold: Common formats that provide ample space for information and visuals.
  • Pamphlets: Typically single-page brochures that are folded.
  • Catalogs: Multi-page brochures that showcase a range of products or services.

Images

Images play a crucial role in both cards and brochures. They can:
  • Enhance Visual Appeal: High-quality images make the content more engaging.
  • Convey Messages: Images can communicate ideas quickly and effectively.
  • Brand Identity: Consistent use of images helps in building and maintaining brand identity

COLOR PALLETTes

Color (color.adobe.com) – Create and explore colour combinations. Adobe Color (color.adobe.com) is a web-based
tool that empowers designers and creatives to craft and explore
captivating colour combinations. This innovative platform simplifies the colour selection
process by offering many features. Users can generate harmonious colour palettes using various colour rules,
extract shades from images, or access pre-designed themes.

There are two main categories of color models: subtractive and additive. An additive color model (like the RGB model) is based on transmitting light. Subtractive color models (like CMYK) are based on reflected light.
